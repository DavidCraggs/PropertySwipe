# PropertySwipe Merseyside Regional Launch Plan
## Loss-Leading Strategy for North West Market Entry

---

## Executive Summary

This document outlines a targeted go-to-market strategy for launching PropertySwipe in the **Merseyside and North West region**, using a **loss-leading pricing model** to rapidly acquire market share before national expansion. The strategy leverages Liverpool's strong rental market fundamentals and targets specific agencies for partnership.

**Launch Region:** Merseyside (Liverpool, Wirral, Southport, St Helens, Knowsley)
**Strategy:** Loss-leading free tier + aggressive early adopter discounts
**Timeline:** 6-month intensive regional focus
**Target:** 100 agencies, 1,500+ properties listed
**Investment Required:** £150,000 (pre-revenue runway)

## Loss-Leading Pricing Strategy

### Philosophy

**"Sacrifice Year 1 revenue to dominate the market and build switching costs."**

The property management software market has high switching costs once agents integrate PropertySwipe into their daily workflows. By offering aggressive pricing initially, we can:
1. Acquire customers at minimal CAC
2. Build case studies and testimonials
3. Create network effects (more properties = more renters = more value)
4. Establish market presence before competitors react
5. Collect invaluable product feedback

#### Free Pioneer Tier (First 10 agencies)

**Price:** £0 for 12 months
**Target:** Tier 1 Strategic Partners

## Risk Assessment - Regional Launch

### High Risks

#### 1. Low Agency Adoption Despite Free Offer

**Risk Level:** HIGH (40%)
**Impact:** Failed proof of concept, wasted runway

**Mitigation:**
- Qualify agencies before offering free tier
- Set minimum commitment requirements
- Build personal relationships with key decision-makers
- Offer implementation support and training
- Create FOMO with limited slots

**Contingency:**
- Pivot to direct-to-landlord model
- Reduce scope to smaller number of premium partnerships
- Consider acquiring struggling local agency

---

#### 2. Pilot Agencies Don't Provide Testimonials

**Risk Level:** MEDIUM (35%)
**Impact:** Can't build case studies for expansion

**Mitigation:**
- Include testimonial requirement in pilot agreement
- Make it easy (video call, written options)
- Offer incentives for participation
- Build genuine value so testimonials are authentic
- Capture data for objective case studies

**Contingency:**
- Use data-driven case studies instead (anonymized if necessary)
- Focus on metrics: void periods, time-to-let, renter satisfaction

---

#### 3. Churn at End of Free Period

**Risk Level:** HIGH (45%)
**Impact:** Year 1 investment doesn't convert to Year 2 revenue

**Mitigation:**
- Build deep integration (data, workflows, habits)
- Create features only available to paying customers
- Offer significant discount for early commitment
- Demonstrate clear ROI with monthly reports
- Make data export possible but inconvenient
- Build community and support that adds value

**Contingency:**
- Accept 50-60% renewal as success
- Focus expansion efforts on proven segments
- Use learnings for national pricing strategy

---

#### 4. Competitor Reaction

**Risk Level:** MEDIUM (30%)
**Impact:** Price war, accelerated feature development by incumbents

**Mitigation:**
- Move fast, lock in relationships before reaction
- Focus on features competitors can't quickly match (RRA 2025)
- Build brand loyalty and community
- Patent/trademark where possible
- Create exclusive partnerships

**Contingency:**
- Emphasize differentiation, not price
- Pivot to underserved segments
- Consider acquisition or partnership

---

#### 5. Technical Issues at Scale

**Risk Level:** MEDIUM (25%)
**Impact:** Reputation damage, agency churn

**Mitigation:**
- Load test before major onboarding
- Staged rollout (don't onboard 50 agencies at once)
- 24-hour support commitment for pilots
- Automatic error monitoring and alerting
- Regular backup and disaster recovery testing

**Contingency:**
- Rollback procedures ready
- Credit/extension offers for affected agencies
- Transparent communication about issues

---
